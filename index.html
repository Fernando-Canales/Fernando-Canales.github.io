Intro

I am a PhD student at LESIA in the Meudon site of the Paris Obsevatory. My PhD project focuses on the study and development of correction algorithms for the CCDs of PLATO space mission. Although highly efficient, CCD detectors are subject to a number of physical phenomena that induce flux variations that disrupt scientific measurements. At the same time, I am involved in the development of different aperture models for PLATO. By testing several aperture configurations, we look for the best compromise in terms of SNR and the smallest ammount of false positives throughout the life time of the spacecraft.

I began my scientific formation back in Mexico, at the University of Guanajuato (UG). I did my Bachelor's degree in Physics at the Science and Engineering Division of that University. Later on, I obtained my Master's degree in Astrophysics at the Astronomy Department of that University.

In 2022 I moved to Paris to start my PhD in Astronomy and Astrophysics. My PhD is splited in two: The first half is taking place at Paris Observatory, site Meudon. The second half will take place at Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research, in Göttingen.

RESEARCH

My research is focused in the development of correction algorithms for the CCDs of PLATO space mission. These algorithms will ensure the acquisition of solid and reliable scientific measurements for the detection of extra-solar planets via the transit method, as well as in star's characterization via asteroseismology.

Here is my CV, updated as of May 29th of 2022.

PLATO

The PLAnetary Transits and Oscillations of Stars (PLATO) space mission has been adopted by ESA as the M3 mission of the Cosmic Vision program (launch scheduled for late 2026). Its main objective is to detect and characterize a large number of extra-solar planets, primarly earth-sized planets located in the habitable zone of Sun-like stars.

EXOPLANETS

I am also interested in the formation and characterization of extrasolar planets. I have been involved in research projects regarding the modelling of protoplanetary disks and in the characterization of Ultra Short Period (USP) planets. Although my PhD project is focused on instrumentation, my goal is to implement all the knowledge that I would get during my PhD towards concrete ways of improving for the characterization of exoplanets, specially with the transit method.
